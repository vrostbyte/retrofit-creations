> **No fancy equipment needed.** A phone camera and a window is all it takes.

## PART 1: Quick Photography Tips

### The Setup (5 minutes, costs nothing)

1. **Find a window.** Natural light is your best friend. A window with indirect sunlight (not direct harsh sun) gives you soft, even lighting. Morning or overcast days are ideal.

2. **Make a backdrop.** Tape a piece of white poster board to a wall and let it curve down onto a table — this creates a seamless white background. No visible edges, no distracting textures. You can also use a large sheet of white paper, a white bedsheet, or even a clean white cutting board for small items.

3. **Phone camera settings.** Turn OFF the flash (flash creates harsh shadows). Turn ON portrait mode if your phone has it — this slightly blurs the background and makes the product pop. If your phone has a 2x zoom, use it instead of getting super close (reduces distortion).

4. **Stable hands or a stand.** Rest your elbows on the table while shooting, or lean your phone against something. A $10 phone tripod from Amazon is worth it if you're doing a lot of shots, but not required.

### Taking the Shot

- **Fill the frame.** The product should take up 60-80% of the image. Don't leave tons of empty space.
- **Shoot straight-on or at a slight angle (about 30-45 degrees).** Avoid extreme angles unless going for an artsy look.
- **Take 3-5 photos per product** from different angles: front, slight angle, close-up detail, and one showing it in context (e.g., a Zyn tin in someone's hand, a keychain on a set of keys, a bracket installed on a car).
- **Keep the background clean.** No clutter, no random stuff in the background. Just the product.
- **Show scale.** For small items, having a hand holding the product helps customers understand the size.

### Quick Editing (Optional)

Most phones have built-in photo editors. Two quick adjustments that make a big difference:

1. **Brightness:** Bump it up slightly so the white background is actually white (not gray)
2. **Crop:** Trim any extra space so the product is centered and prominent

That's it. Don't over-filter, don't add borders, don't use Instagram-style effects. Clean and simple.

### Photo Specs for the Website

- **Format:** JPG or PNG (your phone's default is fine)
- **Minimum resolution:** 1000 x 1000 pixels (most phone cameras shoot way higher than this — you're fine)
- **Orientation:** Square is ideal for product grids. If you shoot rectangular, we can crop.
- **File size:** Under 5MB per image (phone photos are usually 2-4MB, so you're good)
- **File naming:** Name them something descriptive when you send them. Example: `zyn-tin-red-flame-design-front.jpg` is way better than `IMG_4837.jpg`

## PART 3: How to Send Photos to Josh

Option A (easiest): Create a shared Google Drive folder called "RC Website Photos" and dump everything in there. Organize by category if you can.

Option B: Send via email in batches. Try to keep each email under 25MB total (about 5-8 photos per email).

Option C: AirDrop or share via a messaging app if you're both on phones.

**When you name files, use this pattern:**
```
[category]-[product-name]-[angle].jpg

Examples:
zyn-tin-red-front.jpg
zyn-tin-blue-custom-logo-detail.jpg
keychain-mountain-design-on-keys.jpg
automotive-bracket-69-camaro-installed.jpg
event-booth-setup-carlsbad-show.jpg
```

This helps us match photos to the right products in the database.
