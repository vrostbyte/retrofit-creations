Photography Tips & Best Practices

Taking clean product photos doesn't require expensive DSLR cameras or studio lighting. Just follow this easy setup to make your phone photos look premium and consistent.

The Setup (5 Minutes, Costs Nothing)

1

Find a Window

Natural light is your best friend. Choose a window with **indirect sunlight** (not direct bright sun) for soft, even lighting. Morning or overcast days are best.

2

Make a Backdrop

Tape a piece of white poster board to a wall and curve it down onto a table to create a **seamless, edge-free background**.

3

Camera Settings

Turn **OFF** the flash. Turn **ON** portrait mode (slightly blurs background). If your phone has a **2x optical zoom**, use it and step back to prevent distortion.

Taking the Shot: Angle Checklist

  • **Fill the frame:** The product should occupy **60-80%** of the space. Don't leave huge empty margins.
  • **Keep angles clean:** Shoot straight-on or at a slight downward angle (30-45°). Avoid extreme top-down or bottom-up angles.
  • **Provide variety:** Take **3-5 photos per product** (Front hero shot, 45-degree angle, close-up details showing engravings, and showing it in context, like a keychain on car keys).
  • **Keep backdrop clean:** Clean the poster board regularly. Wipe away dust or fingerprints on Zyn tins before taking photos.
  • **Scale reference:** For small keychains or accessories, hold them in your hand for one shot to show the real size.

Quick Editing & Website Specs

You can use your phone's built-in editor to make two fast tweaks: increase **brightness** (so gray backgrounds become crisp white) and **crop** to center the item. Do not use creative filters or borders.

Requirement Spec Value Notes
File Format JPG or PNG Your phone's default is perfect
Resolution Min. 1000 x 1000 pixels Modern phones shoot far higher than this
Crop Ratio Square (1:1 ratio) Ideal for product grids; we can crop later if needed
File Size Under 5MB per image Standard mobile shots run between 2-4MB

Filename Rules Quick Reference

  • Always use **all lowercase letters**.
  • Replace spaces or underscores with **hyphens (-)**.
  • Strip out special symbols (like `#`, `&`, `@`, `!`, `"`).
  • Keep names clean. Example: `zyn-tin-red-front.jpg` is far better than `IMG_94382.JPG` or `Red Zyn Tin!.jpg`.

How to Send Photos to Josh

Option B: Email in Batches

Email images directly to Josh. Keep batches under **25MB total** (around 5-8 photos per email) to ensure they are received.

Option C: Direct Share

If meeting in person, AirDrop is fastest. You can also send them via messaging apps (Telegram/Signal/etc.) if files are sent as uncompressed documents.
